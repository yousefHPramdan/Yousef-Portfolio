# PART 36 — DISCOVERY CALL SYSTEM (FULL SCRIPT)

## Why You Need a Discovery Call System
Most freelancers wing it on calls. They answer questions instead of leading conversations.
A scripted call does 4 things:
1. Qualifies the client (can they afford you, is the project real?)
2. Surfaces the real problem (not just what they said in the brief)
3. Positions you as the expert (you ask the questions experts ask)
4. Sets up your proposal to close (you frame scope on the call, confirm via proposal)

## Before the Call (5 min prep)

```
Checklist:
□ Run PageSpeed on their store (if Shopify)
□ Open their store on mobile — note 1–2 obvious issues
□ Check their IG/LinkedIn — note their product niche + brand tone
□ Have Loom open (record the call if permitted)
□ Have your proposal template open (fill during call)
```

---

## Call Script (30 minutes)

### Opening (2 min)

```
"Hey [NAME], great to connect.
Before I answer any questions — can I take 5 minutes to understand your situation first?
That way anything I say will actually be relevant to you.
Cool?"

[They say yes — everyone says yes.]
```

---

### Discovery Questions (15 min)

**Business Context:**
```
"Tell me about the store — what do you sell and who buys it?"

"How long have you been on Shopify?"

"What does success look like for this project in 3 months?
Like, if we did a call 3 months from now and you were happy —
what would have changed?"
```

**The Real Problem:**
```
"What's prompted you to reach out now — why this week and not 3 months ago?"

"Have you tried fixing this yourself or hired someone before?
What happened?"

"What's the biggest frustration with the store right now — 
not the technical thing, but the business outcome that's suffering?"
```

**Scope + Budget (ask directly):**
```
"Do you have a rough budget in mind for this?
Even a range — it helps me know whether to scope a quick fix or a full build."

[If they say "no idea" → ]
"No problem. On Fiverr I do quick fixes from $5.
Full custom builds run $800–$4,000+ depending on scope.
Which end feels closer to what you're thinking?"

"What's the timeline — do you have a launch date or deadline driving this?"
```

**Decision-Making:**
```
"Is it just you making this decision or do you have a partner or team involved?"
[Important: if someone else decides, ask to include them]

"Have you seen other developers or agencies? 
What did you like or not like about what you saw?"
```

---

### Presentation (10 min)

```
"Okay, based on what you've told me — here's how I see it:

The core problem is [THEIR PROBLEM IN THEIR WORDS].
That's causing [BUSINESS IMPACT — sales, bounce, CVR].
The fix is [3-STEP APPROACH].

Here's what I'd do:
Phase 1: [X] — [1 sentence]
Phase 2: [Y] — [1 sentence]
Phase 3: [Z] — [1 sentence]

Rough timeline: [X] days/weeks.
Investment range: $[X]–$[Y].

Does that match what you had in mind?"
```

---

### Close (3 min)

```
"The next step is for me to send you a written proposal 
with exact deliverables, timeline, and price.
I'll have it to you within [24–48] hours.

One thing — I usually take a deposit before starting 
(50% upfront, 50% on delivery) just to lock in the slot.

Does that work for you?"

[If yes] → "Great. I'll send the proposal to [EMAIL] by [TIME]."
[If hesitant] → "Totally fine. Read the proposal first, 
there's no pressure. 
If it makes sense we move forward, if not no hard feelings."
```

---

## Red Flags — When to Walk Away

```
🚩 "We have a very tight budget" (asked before you mentioned price)
🚩 "The last developer disappeared" (might be their fault)
🚩 "We just need a quick fix — nothing complicated" (usually complicated)
🚩 "Can you match the price of [cheaper option]?" (race to the bottom)
🚩 "We're deciding between 10 developers" (you're being used for price comparison)
🚩 They can't answer "what does success look like?" (no clear goal = scope creep)
```

# PART 37 — PRICING PSYCHOLOGY & NEGOTIATION

## The 3-Tier Pricing Rule

Always present 3 options. Never 1.

Why: When clients see 1 price, they ask "is this too expensive?"
When they see 3 prices, they ask "which one fits me best?"

**Template:**
```
Option A — Starter: $[X]
[3 bullet points of what's included]
Best for: [Use case]

Option B — Professional: $[Y] ← most popular
[5 bullet points — includes everything in A + more]
Best for: [Use case]

Option C — Premium: $[Z]
[7 bullet points — the works]
Best for: [Use case]

Most clients choose Option B.
Happy to discuss any of these — just reply or book a 15-min call.
```

---

## The Anchor Technique

Always mention the app/agency cost BEFORE your price.

**Example:**
```
"A Shopify agency would charge $8,000–$15,000 for this project.
I deliver the same quality for $1,800 — because I have lower overhead
and I'm building a portfolio of case studies like yours."
```

**Another version:**
```
"The apps doing this charge $40–$60/month.
Over 3 years, that's $1,440–$2,160.
My one-time snippet costs $24.90 and you own it forever."
```

---

## Handling Common Objections

### "Your price is too high"

```
"I understand. Can I ask — what were you expecting to pay?

[If they give a number significantly lower:]
"I appreciate the honesty. At that price point I can do [REDUCED SCOPE].
What's non-negotiable for you — the full scope or the budget?
Because I can adjust scope to fit the budget, but I can't reduce quality."

[If the gap is small:]
"Here's what I can do — I'll remove [LOWEST VALUE ITEM] from the scope
and bring it to $[ADJUSTED PRICE]. Does that work?"
```

### "I need to think about it"

```
"Of course — what would help you decide?

Is it the price, the scope, the timeline, or something else?

[Usually it's one of these — address it directly]

I'll be honest with you — I have [1–2] project slots available this month.
I'm not trying to pressure you, but if timing matters to you,
it's worth knowing that."
```

### "Can I pay after delivery?"

```
"I understand — that's the safest position from your side.

I work with 50% deposit because it covers my time if a project
falls through (it happens in freelancing).

What I can offer: 30% deposit to start, 40% at midpoint, 30% on delivery.
That protects both of us. Does that work?"
```

### "Can you do it cheaper if I give you more work?"

```
"I appreciate the offer — if there's genuinely more work coming,
let's talk retainer. A monthly retainer gets you a better hourly rate
because I'm allocating dedicated time to your store.

What's the volume of work you're thinking monthly?"
```

---

## When to Raise Your Rate

Raise your Fiverr/Upwork rate when:
- Your order queue is 2+ weeks out
- You're saying no to projects
- 80%+ of clients accept without negotiating
- You hit Level 1 (Fiverr) or Rising Talent (Upwork)

Raise by 30–50% increments, not 10%.
Small raises signal insecurity. Bold raises signal confidence.

# PART 39 — RETAINER CLIENT SYSTEM

## What Is a Retainer?
A monthly agreement where a client pays a fixed fee for a set amount of your time/deliverables per month.

**Why retainers beat one-off projects:**
- Predictable monthly income (no feast/famine cycle)
- You know the store deeply — less ramp-up time per task
- Clients who trust you don't negotiate every project
- One retainer at $1,200/mo = 60% of a $2,000/mo goal

# PART 47 — SCOPE CREEP MANAGEMENT

## What Is Scope Creep?
When a client adds requirements after the scope is agreed — without adjusting payment.
Most freelancers absorb this silently. It kills profitability.

---

## Prevention: The Scope Doc (Send Before Starting)

```
PROJECT SCOPE — [NAME] — [DATE]

INCLUDED:
✓ [Deliverable 1 — specific and measurable]
✓ [Deliverable 2]
✓ [Deliverable 3]
✓ 2 rounds of revisions

NOT INCLUDED:
✗ [Common addition 1 — be specific]
✗ [Common addition 2]
✗ [Common addition 3]
✗ Additional pages or sections beyond those listed

CHANGE REQUESTS:
Any addition to the above scope will be quoted separately.
I'll always inform you before starting additional work.

CONFIRMED BY: [Client name] on [Date]
```

---

## Scripts for Common Scope Creep Situations

### "Can you also add X while you're in there?"

```
"Happy to! That's outside the original scope, 
but I can add it for an additional $[X].
Should take about [X] hours.

Want me to include it in this project or quote it separately?"
```

### "I just realized I need the page in Arabic too"

```
"Totally understandable — bilingual support adds 
roughly [X hours] to the project.
I can add it for $[X] and extend the timeline by [X] days.

Alternatively, I can deliver the English version on time 
and quote the Arabic separately after."
```

### "Can you make one small change after delivery?"

```
If it's within the 14-day support window AND related to the original scope:
"Of course — I'll get that done within [X hours]."

If it's new work:
"That's a new change outside the original delivery.
For small tasks like this I charge $[X] — 
want me to go ahead?"
```

# PART 50 — MINDSET & DAILY OPERATIONS

## The Freelancer's Identity Shift

**Stage 1 — "I need work"**
Chasing every lead, underpricing, afraid to say no.
This is where most freelancers stay forever.

**Stage 2 — "I do good work"**
Quality over volume. Turning down bad-fit clients.
Prices based on value, not fear.

**Stage 3 — "Clients come to me"**
Content, portfolio, and referrals bring inbound.
You choose your projects, not the other way around.

**Stage 4 — "I run a business"**
Systems handle repetitive tasks.
Products and retainers fund your life while you work on interesting problems.

Your goal: move through these stages as fast as possible.
Each stage requires intentional action to reach the next.
